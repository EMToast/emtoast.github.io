Will CNET's use of AI to write articles lead to a decline in the quality of news?

By EMToast Staff
January 15, 2023

CNET, a popular technology news website, has come under fire for its recent use of AI to write more than 75 articles. Many critics argue that this is a dangerous development that threatens to undermine the role of human journalists and devalue the importance of original, well-researched content.

One of the biggest concerns is that AI-generated articles lack the nuance and critical thinking that human journalists bring to their work. They may be able to spit out a quick news story or summary, but they lack the ability to ask important questions, conduct in-depth research, and provide context and analysis. Furthermore, AI-generated articles are often based on pre-existing data and information, which means they may lack the originality and creativity that human journalists bring to their work.

Another concern is that the use of AI to write articles could lead to the loss of jobs for human journalists. As more and more news organizations turn to AI to produce content, the demand for human journalists will decrease, leading to a decline in the number of people working in the field. This would be a tragedy for the industry and for society as a whole, as the role of journalists is to provide important information and hold those in power accountable.

Moreover, AI-generated articles also raises ethical concerns. If a news organization relies on AI to produce a large portion of its content, it raises questions about the accuracy and reliability of the information being presented to the public. If the AI is not programmed to consider ethical implications or biases, it may produce content that is misleading or harmful.

In conclusion, the use of AI to write more than 75 articles by CNET is a cause for concern. While it may be efficient, it lacks the creativity, critical thinking, and ethical considerations that human journalists bring to their work. Furthermore, it may lead to job loss and a decline in the quality of information being presented to the public. It is important for news organizations to consider the long-term implications of their use of AI and ensure that they continue to prioritize the importance of original and well-researched content.
