Inspirational Madlibs: Don't Sweat the Small Stuff

By EMToast Staff
February 2, 2009

 

Using the definitions provided, and your inner spirit guide,  fill out the boxes below with the correct type of word. After that click compose letter, then copy and paste your inspirational words  into the comments.

 

 

Noun: A word that can be used to refer to a person or place or thing. In sentences, nouns generally function as subjects or as objects.
Examples: angel, filth, orusib

 

Verb: A word that shows action or state of being.
Examples: rise, fall, desecrate

 

Adverb: A word that describes or modifies a verb, frequently ending with “-ly.”.
Examples: blindly, faithfully, gracefully

 

Adjective: A word that describes an noun or pronoun.
Examples: deplorable, cancerous, sinister
