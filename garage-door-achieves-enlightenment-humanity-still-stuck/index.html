Garage Door Achieves Enlightenment: Humanity Still Stuck

By EMToast Staff
December 6, 2023

In the quaint town of Peculiar Springs, where the sunsets painted the sky in shades of irony, there stood an unassuming house. Its garage door, however, was anything but ordinary. It was a paradox—a stubborn enigma that defied the laws of mechanics and common sense.

Mr. Archibald Grumblebee, the homeowner, scratched his bald head as he stared at the perplexing contraption. The garage door had a mind of its own. It neither opened nor closed. It hung there, suspended in indecision, like a hesitant moth caught between two porch lights.

The townsfolk gathered to witness this peculiar phenomenon. They brought lawn chairs, popcorn, and their best sarcastic remarks. After all, what else could one do when faced with a garage door that defied its purpose?

The Stuck Door Chronicles became a weekly event. Families picnicked on the grass, children played tag around the immobile door, and teenagers wrote angst-ridden poems about existential garage dilemmas. The local website, EMToast.com, assigned me—the intrepid reporter Anthony J. Booze—to unravel this mystery.

The Investigation Begins

I interviewed Mr. Grumblebee, who had grown fond of the door. “It’s like a silent companion,” he mused. “A metaphor for life’s uncertainties.”

“But what about your car?” I asked.

“Oh, I sold it. Walking is good for the soul,” he replied, adjusting his mismatched socks.

Next, I consulted Professor Ignatius Whimsy, the town’s eccentric physicist. He wore a lab coat adorned with glitter and spoke in rhyming couplets.

“Ah, young Anthony,” he said, stroking his handlebar mustache. “This door exists in a quantum state—simultaneously open and closed. It’s Schrödinger’s Garage, you see.”

“But why?” I pressed.

“Because,” he whispered, “it yearns for purpose. A door that can’t decide its fate is the ultimate existential crisis.”

The Moral Repercussions

As weeks turned into months, the townsfolk pondered the deeper implications. The stuck garage door became a symbol:

  1. The Perils of Indecision: Couples sought counseling, fearing their relationships might end up like the door—neither fully open nor closed. Divorce rates plummeted, but therapy sessions skyrocketed.
  2. The Absurdity of Routine: The local bakery named a pastry after the door: the “Half-Closed Danish.” It was flaky, indecisive, and filled with existential dread.
  3. The Power of Acceptance: Children wrote essays about embracing life’s quirks. The school motto changed to “Be the Garage Door You Wish to See in the World.”
  4. The Zen of Stillness: Meditation classes gathered in the garage, chanting, “Om, ohm, open, close, ohm.”

The Grand Finale

One fateful evening, during a lunar eclipse, the townsfolk held hands around the garage. The door trembled, torn between dimensions. Mr. Grumblebee recited a haiku:

Stuck door, silent muse, Teach us life’s sweet paradox— Embrace the in-between.

And then, with a cosmic sigh, the garage door split into two translucent halves. One side floated upward, the other sank into the earth. The crowd gasped. Was this closure or liberation?

Professor Whimsy grinned. “The door found balance. It’s now both open and closed, everywhere and nowhere.”

As the townsfolk dispersed, I penned my headline for EMToast:

“Garage Door Achieves Enlightenment: Humanity Still Stuck.”

And so, dear reader, remember this: Life’s greatest mysteries often hang in the liminal spaces. Embrace them, for in the absurdity lies our shared humanity.

—Anthony J. Booze, Reporting from Peculiar Springs

Note: The garage door later started a successful podcast called “The Existential Hinge.”
