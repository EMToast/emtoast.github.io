How a 25 Year Old Video Game Changed Everything

By EMToast Staff
February 21, 2015
“Ask the Doctor” where readers ask questions of the world renounced general practitioner Dr. Charles V. Fullerton

Dear Doctor:  Many years ago, when I was in high school the school made us stand and recite the pledge of allegiance.   We were into  video game cartridges and found the pledge a bit boring so we began modifying it to match our personalities.

I made my pledge of allegiance to the Sega Genesis and my friend made the TurboGraphx-16.  I have never regretted my decision to chose Genesis over TurboGraphx, but I feel I made a mistake declaring my allegiance so young. That was about 25 years ago and my pledge has kept me from many other systems such as Super Nintendo, N64, Playstations 1~4, Several Xboxes, Wii and Wii U.  I have never even touched a Gameboy or an Iphone Game? What should I do. I don't have as much time for gaming anymore, but still wonder if it's time for a change -- Pixel72

Dear Pixel72:   You have gone against your country.  I am reporting you to the authorities.  In the meantime, can I borrow your Genesis.  I still have an old M.U.S.H.A cartridge that I'm dying to play again.  If not, I'll just check Virtual Console.  Good luck with your exportation.
